Syndicated Foreign Currency Options

Syndicated loans arise when a project requires too large a loan for a single lender or when a project needs a specialized lender with expertise in a specific asset class. Lenders can distribute risk and participate in financial possibilities that are too huge for their own capital bases by syndicating the loan.
In 2015 and 2016, MAY International successfully raised a total of USD 105 million for AB Bank through a Syndicated loan arrangement against bond with Noor Bank Dubai (now Dubai Islamic Bank) acting as the lead arranger. With this expertise and experience, MAY INT continues to be a market leader in arranging alternative bank facilities.

Structured Financing

Structured finance is a financial instrument available for companies with complex financing needs, which cannot be usually solved with conventional financing methods. Moreover, traditional lenders often do not offer structured financing.
MAY INT assist companies in raising cash through a variety of financial instruments, including term loans, syndicated loans in both local and foreign currency, Collateralized debt obligations (CDOs), hybrid securities, collateralized bond obligations (CBOs), among others.
