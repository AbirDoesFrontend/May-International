Letters of Credit

The primary aim of this instrument is to provide increased assurance to both the buyer and seller of the fulfilment of each party’s obligations in a commercial trade. Due to the nature of international transactions, including factors such as distance, differing laws in each country, language/cultural barriers and difficulty in knowing each party personally, the use of letters of credit has become a very important aspect of international trade.

MAY INT exclusively acts as the country representative of Bank of Huzhou (BOH) to cover end-to-end correspondent banking services of Sino-Bangla trade amounting to US$16 bn a year. We have an established RMA relationship with 42 Bangladeshi banks in our network. BOH responds to “The Belt and Road” strategy by collaboration with Bangladeshi banks and at the same time helping to solve exporters’ difficulties in financing.

Trade Loan Facilities and Buyers Credit

Trade loans work as fully revolving credit facilities that assist a business in funding its operations between the time it must pay for purchased products and the time it gets revenues from the sale of those goods. Trade loans are an important and well-established trade finance technique – enabling finance to be provided until payment for goods is received, particularly suited to wholesalers and manufacturers.

MAY INT can structure trade financing arrangements against Upas (Usance Payable at Sight) or discounting to suit your trading cycle and both regular and one-off financing needs. With the documentary credit UPAS clause, you can benefit from payment terms while allowing your supplier to be paid quickly. LC commission is charged by the issuing bank on a quarterly basis is against the LC settled amount.

What can trade loans do for you?

  • Facilitate foreign currency funding for trade credits to importers in Bangladesh at competitive pricing for extended period as per the operating cycle.
  • Can accommodate both high-volume transactions and can be adapted to your trading cycle.
  • The product facilitates the payment of a Usance LC on sight to the exporter or seller, while payment settlement from the importer or buyer to the LC issuing bank occurs on the due date as per the contract terms.
  • Can enhance your reputation with suppliers by allowing you to accept shorter payment terms.
  • Could reduce your overall borrowing costs.

Factoring and Receivable Finance

A factor acts as an intermediary agent that provides cash or financing to companies by purchasing their accounts receivables. It is a tailor-made package of services designed to secure your debt collection and improve your cash flow, thereby offering you a competitive edge in your markets, both domestic and abroad.

MAY INT partners with London Forfaiting Company and its subsidiary Stenn, which is the largest and fastest-growing online platform for financing small and medium sized businesses engaged in international trade. They are both based in London, provides financing services in 74 countries and is backed by financial giants like HSBC, Barclays, Natixis and many others.

* All companies with a stable portfolio offering credit terms up to 180 days to corporate buyers can make use of factoring services. The receivables must result from completely fulfilled contracts by the seller. 

Standby Letter of Credit (SBLC)

An SBLC acts as a safety net for the payment of a shipment of physical goods or completed service to the seller, in the event something unforeseen prevents the buyer from making the scheduled payments to the seller. In such a case, the SBLC ensures the required payments are made to the seller after fulfilment of the required obligations.

Sustainability

International trade is an engine for economic growth and development, but it also accounts for some 80% of the world’s carbon emissions. This hub aims to report on how economies are tackling the challenge of meeting the targets of the 2016 Paris Agreement, which set a path for signatory countries to reduce greenhouse emissions.

We also aim to monitor the UN’s 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development recognizes international trade as an engine for inclusive economic growth and poverty reduction, and an important means to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
